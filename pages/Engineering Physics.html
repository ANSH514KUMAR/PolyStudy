Engineering Physics


Engineering Physics in a polytechnic provides foundational physics knowledge (mechanics, optics, magnetism, modern physics) with practical applications, bridging school physics to engineering by teaching how to use physical principles to solve real-world problems in electronics, mechanics, and materials, preparing students for various technical roles. It covers basic concepts like units, motion, waves, and solid-state physics, often including lasers and fiber optics to build core understanding for diplomas in engineering.
